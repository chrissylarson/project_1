Great Lakes Restoration Initiative

the

importance

The Great Lakes offer a distinct historic, economic, and cultural identity to the northern region of the United States. Beyond tangible benefits, the beauty of the Great Lakes offer special significance to the residents of the Midwest and Canada.

The Great Lakes in and of itself are home to 13 of the region’s national parks– an astonishing number that contribute to the local tourism economy and adds a tremendous amount of value to the area for its residents.

the

threat

The national park sites on the Great Lakes and its watershed face many threats, thus The Great Lakes Restoration Initiative formed in 2010, with a goal to “battle invasive species, failing water levels, eroding shorelines and contaminated tributaries.”

But now, the Trump administration

wants to eliminate this funding entirely.

the

impact

Nearly 3,000 restoration projects have been funded by GLRI. It has improved water quality, prevented beach closings, and fought off invasive species from advancing into our lakes and streams.

Now’s the time to help save the Great Lakes.
